About Me

I am a programmer born in Canada and have moved all over Alberta and British Columbia, when I graduated from the university of British Columbia with my bachelors degree in computer science in the summer of 2018. From there I have worked remotely for a small dedicated startup and a large multinational company and have learned much from them, specifacally in the areas of full stack web development and mobile development for Andoid and IOS, as well as different indusry standards and practises.
I am always looking to grow, improve and try new things. I find there is nothing better to me than making a piece of interactive media, from a nice visual design, to hooking up the background logic and showing it off to people, after you put in the effort to make it as robust, efficent and visually pleasing as possible. When I am not working, learning on my own time or working on personal projects, I love to go on hikes with my dog, hang out with my friends or settle down with a nice book.

Projects

Business Infusions:
Developed a mobile version of the signature Business Infusions product, HVMS or Hospital and Veterinary Management System, which handles a wide variety of functionality from medical records to invoicing, which were successfully implemented in the mobile enviroment. Mobile app was developed for Android and IOS phones / tablets.
Stagehand:
Stagehand is a web based SaaS system for connecting Artists and Venues together to book, promote and perform live events, as well as being a hub for seeing what local events are happening when and where. We help Venues such as coffee shops, bars and even international airports find local talent, organize a schedule for multiple events, manage contract agreements, promote events via our site and web widgets, as well as handle payouts to streamline the process.

IoT Communication System:
Using MQTT, myself and a small team were able to collaborate and organize sensor data from advanced water dispensary devices in remote locations around the world in a easy to use website, which notified relevant users of machine errors or discrepencys, and displaying the sensor data on graphs in real-time, which made maintaining, viewing and verifying machine data easy for the client.

Interactive Museum Nature Exhibit:
Using a large touch screen device, myself and a team of artists created a series of 3D eviroments and scenarios for users to go through on the topic of connectivity in nature. Some scenarios included planting a bee friendly garden, navigating a lake filled with danger caused by humans and exploring a true to life map of the Okanagon region and its animals.
Augmented Reality Guided Tour:
Working together with the client on a research project, I created a app using Google Tango technology that would track the user location through both GPS and also scanning the enviroment for extra precision, which allowed us to do many cool things, such as having arrows on the floor lead to specific areas from any location, and displaying contextual information when pointing the device at a area, such as extra details on a painting.
